cuckoo - big

This is three of my friends' culmination of years of house shows and changing formats.
They came to record in the bedroom live with some 4 track fun and recording improv as well
as last minute lyrics and bass features. Vocals were done after the fact, in the middle of
the room. I think there were also some fireplace overdubs we slipped in too. Favorite track
is Scabs.



don strasburg - don strasburg

The sound of a rebellious band about to
be over with itself, recorded in the record shop (thank you Doug) with what was.
ultimately not enough microphones, but in the end even with all of the rough
edges and tape distortion it remains something we are proud of. I believe
there was only one more show after this one, and then we all quit.



american grandma - superdog

Bass amp in the kitchen, guitar amps in the spare room, drums in the
bedroom, tuned slightly differently each day depending on temperature as
recordings were spread out over several weeks, and we used our regular stuff
amped up through tubes and mixed it in Cubase. Vocals were done quickly
as we were up against Jensen's changing voice.



terremoto - fun at parties

I used to have a Pearl Export 6 piece drum kit. Now picture me, the kit,
four other dudes, all of my material posessions including my bed all in a single
15x15' room in a forest of microphone stands. You can hear us in the quiet
moments of the album and in the buzz of the snare. At one point, my phone went off
but they were cool with keeping it.



xenon superstar - dreams are for babies

This was my first time helping someone else record,
and remains one of my favorite attempts. The band had drums without
cymbals and just a few guitars, so we were able to have fun with microphone placement.
We set two rode condensers at waist length on oppposite sides of the room, mic'd amps
at the cone, and vocals were recorded inside of the closet between coats for dampening.
I also attempted some tape saturation in post, but it spoke for itself without it.



american grandma - sensation / forever

Before there were American Grandma shows with the two of us, there were Logic Pro X files traded
over Google Drive. This album is the result of a virtual band that didn't comunicate in the
same room with the same ideas yet, and everything was recorded separately. Drums out in the living room,
vocals wherever we could set up mics, and an overall confessional style.



friends with benefits

This was a group effort, but I had the privilege of lending lots of gear and mixing to
several of the artists on this tape. Favorite tracks are All Bells and Willem Defoe.
